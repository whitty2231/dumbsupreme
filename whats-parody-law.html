FAQ Is this Supreme? No. Dumb Supreme is not affiliated in any way with VF Corporation (Supreme Parent Company). We are simply using their name and logo for marketing purposes. How is this legal? Short answer - parody law. Can you elaborate? Of course. By adding the word 'dumb', we are technically "making fun" of Supreme, which allows us to use their trademark under a law know as 'fair use'. Fair use is a doctrine that permits use of copyrighted material in a parodical work without permission from the rights holder. it's the same law that allows Weird Al Yankovic to use the music from Michael Jackson's "Beat It" in his parody song "Eat It'. So is this a real business? Yes it is. Although we are fully trendy fashion brand, for legal purposes Dumb Supreme needs to be categorized as a work of parody art. So, in the eyes of the law, our "ecommerce website" is actually a virtual art gallery and the "merchandise" you're buying is considered the art. But that's for our lawyers to worry about. All you need to do is enjoy our funny website. Are you saying Supreme is dumb? Not at all. In fact, we love Supreme and look up to them as role models. Unfortunately, the only way to use their intellectual property under fair use is if we are making fun of them. So the "dumb" comes out of neccessity, not enmity. We hope Supreme can laugh with us.
